6 DELICACIES YOU MUST TRY IN Zamboanga




Local Cuisine


April 7, 2021

Angelo Catungal


Zamboanga is a peninsula blessed with pink and white sand beaches, rich open seas, bird sanctuaries, historical sites, and beautiful parks. Being a city in a peninsula has also allowed Zamboanga City to capitalize on the Philippines’ marine resources. The city is sometimes called the “Sardines Capital of the Philippines,” and much of its economy and local way of life revolves around the industry.









RECOMMENDED FOODS

But like I often say, the best way to capture the culture of any place, no matter how diverse it is, is through its dishes. Zamboanga City’s local lifestyle, sandy beaches, brightly colored vintas, historical landmarks, and natural landscapes can all be tasted in a bite—or at least here, several bites—of their local Chavacano cuisine. Here are some of the best dishes I was able to immerse myself in during my recent visit to Asia’s Latin City:



  1. Tiulah Itum

    2. The first thing that came into my mind when I saw my bowl of Tiulah Itum is “dinuguan.” However, it definitely had a different taste, despite looking a lot like dinuguan.
    The Tiulah Itum is a type of beef-and-goat stew with a broth blackened with roasted coconut. Seasoned with spices and ginger, this bowl sent me on a roller coaster ride on Zamboanga’s flavors.



  2. Kurma

    3. Kurma is a popular dish in the city. It is the local version of curry. My helping of kurma had the best texture and just the right amount of spice, which was probably caused by the careful braising of the meat in coconut milk during the dish’s preparation.




  3. Satti

    4. The local version of barbecue, called Satti, was one of my favorites. The chicken and beef strips were grilled to crisp perfection. It was served with rice cooked inside woven coconut leaves. Like the two previous dishes, this dish was smothered in a thick spicy sauce.




  4. Tetuan Lechon

    5. This dish is very similar to Lechon Cebu. The one we tried was a little bit salty to my taste, so I ate my helping without sauce.



  5. Ensalada Chamba

  6. A flavorful blend of smoky grilled eggplant, mango, and salted egg, the Ensalada Chamba is a dish I could eat at any time of the day. The sweetness of the diced mangoes blended the flavors of the dish so well.



  7. Paella Chavacana

  8. I was super excited to try their local version of paella because I’m such a huge fan. Zamboanga was one of the places where the Spanish colonizers first established a settlement, and the paella is just one of the few influences that they left in the peninsula.






RESTAURANTS



  1. Hacienda del Palmeras Restaurant

    2. Hacienda del Palmeras Hotel & Restaurant is one of the top-rated restaurants in Zamboanga City.



    This restaurant serves various local Filipino dishes, but the spotlight goes to their famous knickerbocker, a type of dessert similar to halo-halo. It is made with various fruits like ripe mangoes and bananas and jelly, topped with strawberry ice cream.
