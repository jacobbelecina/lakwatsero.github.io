6 DELICACIES YOU MUST TRY IN GUIMARAS




Local Cuisine


April 2, 2021

David Leh


The economy of Guimaras relies heavily on the production of mangoes. That is one of the primary reasons why it explicitly prohibits the import of any mango product outside the island. This is for the purpose of protecting the quality of the mangoes from genetic degradation and diseases.








Guimaras Island is home to one of the sweetest varieties of mangoes in the world. For travelers, the mere mention of Guimaras is synonymous to mangoes. During summer months, tourists can enjoy “mango eat-all-you-can” festivities during their Manggahan Festival.

RECOMMENDED FOODS

Those who are planning their vacation in the Philippines will surely have heard about Cebu, the Queen City of the South, and all its heritage attractions and pristine beaches.



  1. Mango Pizza

    2. Mango Pizza should be one of your bucket list! At first you’ll imagine how it taste, but when you try it? Dude! Everything is perfect! I can say its purely mangoes, sweet and it is actually a good flavor for pizza.



  2. Mango Serafina

    3. One of the best snacks during my BIG Tour! It’s crunchy on the outside and chewy on the inside. It has different flavors like the original Mango, Calamansi and that with less sugar.



  3. Mango Beef Bulalo

    4. Mango Beef Bulalo Basically made of beef shank with bone marrow that is richly boiled in water together with several vegetables, bulalo is so savory especially if simmered for long hours.



  4. Mango Bibingka

    5. Bibingka is a type of rice cake traditionally cooked in clay pots lined with banana leaves and topped with sugar, cheese, or grated coconut. In Guimaras, you can find packaged Royal Mango Bibingka which tastes like puto mamon with a sweet filling of mango jam inside.



  5. Mango Jam

  6. Guimaras mangoes are known as one of the sweetest mangoes in the world. These mangoes are the main ingredient of our mango jam.



  7. Mango Ketchup

      Fruit stalls also sell interseting treats and drinks like Chocolate covered Dried mangoes, mango otap, mango wine, mango ketchup.



RESTAURANTS



  1. The Pitstop

    2. The Pitstop is one of the first few that saw beyond mango as a fruit. Opened in 2005, this local diner started as a place for people to grab some quick grub and recharge, just as car racers make a pit stop. With its bright and casual atmosphere and extraordinary menu offerings, The Pitstop eventually became a favorite stomping ground where people take their time to savor every bite and every spoonful of their meals.
