6 DELICACIES YOU MUST TRY IN SIARGAO




Local Cuisine


April 9, 2021

Angelo Catungal


DSummer’s coming up! After a long day chasing waves in the Surf Capital known as Siargao, grab a bite to eat in these island favorites
The cool breeze we all enjoyed during January and February is now gone, and replaced by a scorching heat that only signals the coming of summer. Make summer 2017 a summer to remember in the Surf Capital of the Philippines, Siargao! This island is not only known for awesome waves, yoga retreats, and Instagram-worthy rock pools, but also really good eats. Here are the top 10 must-eat places when visiting Siargao.









RECOMMENDED FOODS

Going to a new country is always exciting, from immersing in the culture, people and not forgetting the food! If you’re not overly adventurous with your food choices but still want to have a taste of the iconic cuisine while in Davao, Philippines, check out our list of top 10 dishes to try!




  1. Coconut Cold Brew

    2. This is perfect for you boujie coffee drinkers! It’s the coconut cold brew at Greenhouse Cafe. It’s cold brew coffee with fresh coconut milk. And it was so. good.



  2. Patatas Bravas, Coconut Cheesecake & Pomada Cocktail

    3. My top favorites are the patatas bravas, coconut cheesecake, and their signature alcohol drink, pomada. I don’t know what’s in it but it’s one of those dangerous drinks where you can’t taste the alcohol. And it’s only 60 pesos. That’s just over a US dollar!




  3. Banana Pancakes, Paninis & Fresh Juice

    4. I’ve always avoided restaurants by the beach because they were tourist traps but tourist trap or not, #6 is Cafe Loka. And it’s really a great location because after I went surfing in Cloud 9, I was so hungry and was so glad Cafe Loka was right there. They’ve got everything from smiley banana pancakes to mini paninis to fresh juice. And if you want to watch the sunrise while you’re here, definitely do it from Cafe Loka.




  4. Smoothie Bowls

    5. They’re most known for their smoothie bowls and while it’s a bit of a wait, it’s totally worth it. The acai bowl is great if you want to be filled up and if you want something on the light side, the green bowl is your best choice.



  5. Halo-Halo & Sizzling Chicken Joy

  6. They have a wealth of delicous foods and my favorites were their halo-halo, sizzling chicken joy, and binakol rice. And don’t forget to say hi to the famous pug! Harana actually turns into a party zone at night.



  7. Bulalo

  8. Bulaloan is a bulalo restaurant and bulalo is a Filipino stew with beef shanks and bone marrow and it’s THE BEST. Squeeze some calamansi to the broth and a few dashes of patis and you’re set. Don’t forget that bone marrow too! That stuff is potently good. Oh, and did I mention they have unli rice?






RESTAURANTS



  1. Bravo

    2. A restaurant that specializes in affordable Spanish and Asian dishes, Bravo is the place to be if you want something flavorful and filling. Fun fact: They serve a number of vegetarian dishes.



    Notable dishes they offer: Slow-Cooked Coconut Pork Curry, Paella Valenciana, Patatas Bravas, Hummus with Pita Bread, Chorizo Iberico
